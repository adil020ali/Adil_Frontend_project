Famous Foods

Amritsari Kulcha

Crispy, leavened bread stuffed with spiced potatoes and baked in traditional tandoor (clay oven), slathered with generous amount of butter or ghee. It's typically served with spicy chole and a side of pickled onions.

Makki di Roti And Sarson Da Saag

A special Punjabi winter dish. Makki di Roti is a flatbread made from cornmeal and Sarson da Saag is a flavourful curry made from mustard greens,often enriched with butter or ghee.

Lassi

A refreshing, thick, and creamy yogurt-based drink, often served in tall glasses with a dollop of fresh cream (malai). You can find it sweet, salty, or flavored (like kesar or peda lassi).

Chole Bhathure

A popular breakfast or brunch option, this dish features fluffy, deep-fried bhature (bread) served with spicy ,pickeled onions and green chillies.

Aloo Tikki Chaat

Crispy potato patties(aloo tikki) are crushed and topped with a medley of chutneys (tamarind, mint), yogurt, spices, and sometimes chole (chickpea curry) and sev (crispy chickpea flour noodles).

Mathi Chole

Mathis are savory,crispy,flaky biscuits or crackers,often made with refined flou.The crispy mathi is typically broken into pieces and then soaked or generously topped with the hot spicy chole.

Golden Temple (Harimandar Sahib)

The Golden Temple, also known as Harimandar Sahib, is the most sacred shrine in Sikhism. Built in the 16th century, the temple is covered in gold and surrounded by the holy Amrit Sarovar (Pool of Nectar).

Key Features:

  • Open from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM daily for Darshan
  • Free community kitchen (Langar) serves 100000+ people daily 24/7
  • All visitors must cover their heads and remove shoes
  • Stunning fireworks at night on the occasion of gurupurab
  • Historical Sikh museum on site

The temple complex also houses the Sikh Museum, Akal Takht (the highest temporal seat of Sikh authority), and a clock tower.

Jallianwala Bagh

Jallianwala Bagh is a public garden in Amritsar that commemorates the massacre of peaceful protesters by British forces on April 13, 1919.

Key Features:

  • Martyrs' Well where many people jumped to escape shooting
  • Bullet marks preserved on the walls
  • Flame of Liberty memorial designed by American architect Benjamin Polk
  • Museum with photographs and documents of the massacre
  • Sound and Light show in the evenings narrating the history

The site serves as an important reminder of India's struggle for independence. Visitors can still see the narrow entrance that was blocked by British troops, trapping the crowd inside.

Wagah Border

Wagah Border is the only road border crossing between India and Pakistan, located 32 km from Amritsar. The daily flag lowering ceremony is a major tourist attraction.

Key Features:

  • Ceremony held daily before sunset (arrive early)
  • Patriotic songs and dance performances by spectators
  • Highly synchronized marching by border security forces
  • Seating capacity for about 25,000 people
  • VIP seating available (reserve in advance)

The ceremony represents the rivalry and cooperation between India and Pakistan. Soldiers from both countries perform an elaborate drill ending with a simultaneous lowering of both flags.

Durgiana Temple

Durgiana Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Durga,its origins are traced back to the 16th century, the current structure was built in 1921, inspired by the architectural style of the Golden Temple.

Key Features:

  • Open from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM daily
  • Similar design to Golden Temple but with Hindu deities
  • Sacred tank surrounding the temple
  • Special aarti ceremonies in mornings and evenings
  • Navratri festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm

The temple complex also has shrines dedicated to Lakshmi and Narayan. The architecture includes marblework and intricate silver doors.

Gobindgarh Fort

Gobindgarh Fort is a historic military fort that showcases Punjab's martial history. Built in 1760, it has been restored and opened to the public as a heritage site on 10th february 2017.

Key Features:

  • The fort is open from 10:00 AM to 10:00 PM daily.
  • The entry fee for Indian nationals ranges from ₹180 to ₹649, while for foreign nationals, it's ₹375 to ₹1000. These prices may vary based on the specific attractions and shows included in the ticket.
  • Seven museums with artifacts from Sikh and British periods
  • Live cultural shows and martial arts demonstrations
  • Seven museums with artifacts from Sikh and British periods
  • Live cultural shows and martial arts demonstrations
  • Toshakhana (Weapons Repository) with ancient Sikh arms
  • Sher-e-Punjab 7D show about Maharaja Ranjit Singh
  • Reconstructed bazaar with handicrafts and live performances

The fort was once used to protect treasures, including the Kohinoor diamond. Visitors can experience light and sound shows telling Punjab's history.

Ram Tirath

Ram Tirath is an ancient temple complex believed to be the ashram of Rishi Valmiki where Sita gave birth to Luv and Kush. The site dates back to the Ramayana period.

Key Features:

  • Sacred pond where Sita is said to have bathed after childbirth
  • Ancient temples dedicated to Valmiki, Lakshman, and Hanuman
  • Annual Ram Tirath Mela (fair) during Kartika Purnima
  • Archaeological excavations have revealed pottery and coins from 2000 years ago
  • Quiet, spiritual atmosphere away from city crowds

The site includes a tank, temple ruins, and memorials. Devotees believe the pond's water has healing properties and take holy dips.

Maharaja Ranjit Singh Museum

The Maharaja Ranjit Singh Museum is housed in the summer palace of the Sikh emperor and showcases artifacts from the Sikh Empire period (1799-1849).

Key Features:

  • The museum is open from 10 AM to 5 PM, Tuesday to Sunday, and is closed on Mondays.
  • The entry fee of INR 40 for adults and INR 20 for children (5 years and older). Children under 5 years old can enter for free, according to Holidify
  • Original weapons, paintings and manuscripts from the Sikh era
  • Life-size portrait of Maharaja Ranjit Singh on horseback
  • Galleries covering political, military and cultural history
  • Golden throne replica and miniature portraits of Sikh royalty
  • Original coins and armory from the Sikh period

The museum provides insight into the life of one of Punjab's greatest rulers. The palace itself is an example of Sikh architecture with beautiful frescoes.
